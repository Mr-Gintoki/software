Select Vertical Farming

Hydroponics, aeroponics, and aquaponics are innovative farming techniques that enable plant growth in soilless environments. They are part of a broader spectrum of agriculture technologies designed to optimize water usage, increase yields, and allow cultivation in areas where traditional farming is challenging or impossible.

Hydroponic

Hydroponics is a method of growing plants without soil, using mineral nutrient solutions in an aqueous solvent. Plants may be grown with their roots in the nutrient solution alone or in an inert medium, such as perlite, gravel, or coconut husk.

Aeroponic

Aeroponics is a process wherein plants are grown in an air or mist environment without the use of soil or an aggregate medium. The roots hang in the air and are periodically misted with a nutrient-rich solution.

Aquaponic

Aquaponics combines aquaculture (raising fish or other aquatic animals) with hydroponics (growing plants without soil) in a symbiotic environment.
